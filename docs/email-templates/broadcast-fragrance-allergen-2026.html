Fragrance allergen disclosure: 24 now, 81 coming

Hey there —

Quick heads up. Health Canada's fragrance allergen disclosure rules are now in effect, and the list is expanding. If your products use essential oils or fragrance oils, this affects your CNF filings and labels.

Disclosure thresholds: 0.001% in leave-on products, 0.01% in rinse-off products. Below these, the allergen can stay grouped under "Parfum".

Three dates to know

In effect

April 12, 2026

All cosmetics sold in Canada must individually disclose 24 fragrance allergens above the threshold.

Coming

August 1, 2026

Newly-marketed cosmetics must disclose the expanded list of 81 fragrance allergens.

Existing products

August 1, 2028

Existing products already on the market must comply with the 81-allergen list.
